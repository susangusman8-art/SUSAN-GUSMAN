Why is everything done through email? +
Email keeps every conversation documented, searchable, and accountable. When we communicate in writing, there are no misunderstandings, no forgotten agreements, and no vague promises. You always have a full record of what was discussed, decided, and delivered. It is the most professional and transparent way to work — and the most respectful of your time.
How do I get started right now? +
Send an email to susangusman8@gmail.com. Introduce yourself and your book. Tell Susan your genre, where you are in your publishing journey, what challenges you are facing, and what success looks like to you. She will respond with a personalized assessment and a clear path forward.
Do you work with first-time authors? +
Absolutely. First-time authors are some of the best clients to work with because building a strong foundation from day one makes everything that follows more powerful. Whether you are pre-launch, just launched, or trying to revive a book that missed its moment, Susan can help.
What genres do you work with? +
Fiction, non-fiction, self-help, business, memoir, children's books, thriller, romance, fantasy, personal development, and more. If you wrote a book and want the right readers to find it, Susan can build a strategy specifically for your genre and your audience.
How quickly can I expect to see results? +
Most authors begin seeing meaningful improvements in visibility and engagement within 4 to 8 weeks of implementing the strategy. The timeline depends on your starting point, your genre, and how consistently the plan is executed. Susan sets realistic expectations from the start and documents every milestone along the way.
Is this only for self-published authors? +
No. Susan works with self-published, hybrid, and traditionally published authors. If you want to take a more active, strategic role in your book's visibility and sales, Susan can help you do that regardless of how your book was published.
What makes Susan's approach different from others? +
Three things stand out. First, everything is fully customized — no templates, no generic advice. Second, everything runs through documented email, meaning full transparency and accountability at all times. Third, the focus is on sustainable, long-term visibility — not short-lived spikes that collapse after launch week.
My book was published months ago. Is it too late? +
It is never too late. Many of Susan's most dramatic turnarounds came months or even years after a book's original publication. A full audit and a fresh strategy can completely change a book's trajectory at any stage. If the book is worth reading — and it is — it is worth strategizing for.
